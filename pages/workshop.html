Workshop: ''ProbNum & Friends''

The workshop ''ProbNum & Friends'' will take place on the 29th of March, 2023. It's a one-day event with talks and discussions revolving around probabilistic numerical methods. The workshop is open to all registered participants. here.

Confirmed Speakers

Carl Henrik Ek @carlhenrikek

Cambridge University ➔ Website

Before joining the Computer Lab in Cambridge Carl Henrik was a Senior Lecturer at the University of Bristol, prior to this he was an Assistant Professor in Machine Learning at the Royal Institute of Technology (KTH) in Stockholm. He did his postdoctoral research at University of California at Berkeley and PhD is from Oxford Brookes University. He spent two years of his PhD at the University of Manchester where he was a research assistant in the Machine Learning and Optimisation group and a further six months at University of Sheffield as a visitor in the Machine Learning group. His supervisors during his PhD where Professor Neil Lawrence and Professor Phil Torr and during his post-doc he worked under Professor Trevor Darrell and Professor Raquel Urtasun.

Fatemeh Yaghoobi @zhrYaghoobi

Aalto University ➔ Website

Fatemeh is a PhD candidate at Aalto University, Finland, under the supervision of Prof. Simo Särkkä. Her current research involves developing parallel-in-time methods for inference in nonlinear state-space models. Prior to this, she was a member of the Advance Process Automation and Control Research Group at K. N. Toosi University of Technology (KNTU), Iran, working on inference in systems with integrated measurements. She holds MSc and BSc in electrical engineering from KNTU and Shiraz University of Technology, respectively.

Jon Cockayne #probnumschool

University of Southampton ➔ Website

Jon studied Mathematics at Imperial College London, before working in industry for 6 years at RBS, Barclays Capital and Warwick Analytics. He then completed a PhD in Statistics at the University of Warwick under Mark Girolami. The title of his thesis was "Bayesian Probabilistic Numerical Methods". After completing his PhD, he spent two years as a Postdoctoral Research Assistant at the Alan Turing Institute before being appointed as a Lecturer at the University of Southampton. His research interests are in statistical computing and Bayesian methods, particularly when applied to problems from numerical analysis.

Masha Naslidnyk @MNaslidnyk

University College London ➔ Website

Masha is a PhD student at the Foundational AI CDT at University College London, advised by Dr. François-Xavier Briol and Dr. Carlo Ciliberto. Prior to starting her PhD, she was a Machine Learning Scientist at Amazon Research in Cambridge, where she worked on Alexa question answering (2015-2019), and then on Gaussian processes for supply chain emulation (2019-2021). She graduated from Part III in Pure Mathematics at the University of Cambridge in 2014. Her current research is on robust inference in conditional probability models.

Toni Karvonen #probnumschool

University of Helsinki ➔ Website

Toni is an Academy of Finland Postdoctoral Researcher in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Helsinki, Finland. His research is currently funded by the Academy project "Scalable, adaptive and reliable probabilistic integration". Previously he has been a Research Fellow on the Programme for Data-Centric Engineering at the Alan Turing Institute, London, UK, where he worked with Chris Oates and Mark Girolami (2020–2021); he has been a Doctoral Student and Postdoctoral Researcher supervised by Simo Särkkä in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Automation at Aalto University, Espoo, Finland (2016–2020). He completed his doctoral degree in 2019. He was a Student in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at the University of Helsinki, Finland (2010–2015).

Emilia Magnani #probnumschool

University of Tübingen, University of Genoa ➔ Website

Emilia is a Phd student at the University of Tübingen advised by Philipp Hennig. She is also part of the ELLIS PhD program, and spent part of her PhD in Genoa working with her co-supervisor, Lorenzo Rosasco. Before starting her PhD, she graduated with an MSc in Mathematics at ETH Zurich in 2019 and worked a year as a tutor at AIMS South Africa. Her research is on the broad topics of probabilistic numerics, Gaussian processes and differential equations.
