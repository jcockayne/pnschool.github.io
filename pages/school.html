Spring School: Probabilistic Numerics

The Spring School will take place on the 27th and 28th of March, 2023. It will be a two-day event with talks, lectures, and discussions on the topic of probabilistic numerical methods. The school is open to all registered participants.

Confirmed Speakers

Roman Garnett #probnumschool

Washington University || Bayesian Optimization ➔ Website

Roman is an Associate Professor at the Department of Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) at Washington University in St. Louis. He has been a leader in the Bayesian optimization community since 2011, when he co-founded a long-running workshop on the subject at the NeurIPS conference. His research focus is developing Bayesian methods – including Bayesian optimization – for automating scientific discovery, an effort supported by an NSF CAREER award. Roman wrote a book on Bayesian Optimization.

Simo Särkkä @simosarkka

Aalto University || Bayesian Filtering and Smoothing ➔ Website

Simo is an Associate Professor in Aalto University and an Adjunct Professor with tampere University and LUT University. He is also a Fellow of European Laboratory for Learning and Intelligent Systems (ELLIS), and he is the leader of AI Across Fields (AIX) program and AI for Health SIG in Finnish Center for Artificial Intelligence (FCAI). From 2010 to 2013 he worked as a Senior Researcher with the Department of Biomedical Engineering and Computational Science (BECS) at Aalto University, Finland, and also held the position of Academy Research Fellow for 2013-2018. Simo authored books on Bayesian Filtering and Smoothing and Applied Stochastic Differential Equations.

John Cunningham #probnumschool

Columbia University || Numerical Linear Algebra, Gaussian Processes ➔ Website

John is a Professor of Statistics at Columbia University and an investigator at the Zuckerman Mind Brain Behavior Institute and Center for Theoretical Neuroscience. Prior to Columbia, he was a research fellow in the Machine Learning Group at Cambridge. He has a Ph.D. in electrical engineering from Stanford and an undergraduate degree in computer science from Dartmouth. He has also worked with and advised a number of companies. Together with Philipp Hennig and Mike Osborne, John organised the first workshop on probabilistic numerics at Neurips 2012.

Tim Sullivan #probnumschool

University of Warwick || Uncertainty Quantification ➔ Website

Tim is an Associate Professor in Predictive Modelling in the Mathematics Institute and School of Engineering at the University of Warwick. He is also a Turing Fellow at the Alan Turing Institute. He has wide interests in uncertainty quantification the broad sense, understood as the meeting point of numerical analysis, applied probability and statistics, and scientific computation. Tim wrote a book on Uncertainty Quantification.
